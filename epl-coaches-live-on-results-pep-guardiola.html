'EPL Coaches Live On Results'- Pep Guardiola

Manchester City manager, Pep Guardiola, has taken the pleasure to walk his former apprentice Erik Ten Hag through how the EPL works for coaches.

Naija News recall that his predecessors throughout a difficult eight-year span, David Moyes, Louis van Gaal, Jose Mourinho, and Ole Gunnar Solskjaer have all been fired at Old Trafford.

Ahead of the Manchester Derby today, the Spaniard acknowledged that the medals and trophies he has won for his team have kept him as the manager of Manchester City to date.

“If you cannot win with the big clubs, you don’t get time. Erik knows, I know, everybody knows, that managers all depend on results.

“I am still here at City because we won in the past. Otherwise, I would not be here. Erik knows it – and all the managers who were there before Erik and are no longer there, know it.”

“If you don’t win, then you’re in trouble,” Pep stated.

What to expect from Manchester Derby?

Manchester City has been in excellent form this season, sitting second behind Arsenal. Last time out, the Spaniard’s men defeated wolves, and they hope to do so again on Sunday in the 188th Manchester Derby.

Meanwhile, Erik Ten Hag’s side rose from a poor start impressively, winning their last four games in the top flight.
