Uganda: Police Boss Wants VIP Vehicles Fixed with Special Tracking Cameras to Enhance Security

  • President Yoweri Museveni has accused police of negligence in the attempted assassination of Works and Transport Minister Katumba Wamala
  • Paul Lokech admitted that there was laxity by the police force in the attempted murder of Katumba Wamala in which his daughter and driver lost their lives
  • Wamala was shot in the morning of Tuesday, June 1, at Kisota road, Kisaasi, in the capital, Kampala

Uganda's Deputy Inspector General of Police Paul Lokech has said there is need to have vehicles ferrying VIPs installed with special tracking cameras.

"There should be cameras on the vehicles [of VIPs] that would be sending instant signals in case of any action or any act of attack, on the VIP security," said Lokech.

This is after President Yoweri Museveni accused police of negligence in the attempted assassination of Works and Transport Minister Katumba Wamala.

Police laxity

Lokech admitted that there was laxity by the police force in the attempted murder of Katumba in which his daughter and driver lost their lives.

Police have now taken full responsibility blaming their allied squads.

"Our teams of the foot patrols, motorcycles, and other emergency patrols were not alert, and this is a fact. There was a communication gap," said Lokech.

Addressing the press at the police headquarters in Nagullu, Lokech noted that because of the lack of proper communication and coordination, the incident lasted for over 40 minutes.

Delay in some response

Still, not even the police post that is metres away could respond, allowing the assailants time to get away.

This, according to the police boss, will not be tolerated in the future.

"There was some delay in some response. This one we have already observed, and measures are being put in place to effectively ensure that we close on those loopholes that we have found out," said Lokech.

Police standing orders

As per the police standing orders, a police officer is assumed on duty 24/7 by the latest shoot of Katumba. Some police officers are never on duty, while some on duty never pay attention to what they are supposed to do.

"As much as our CCTV surveillance cameras would give us real-time intelligence, there is a need for that coordination to all those people who need to act and execute. We have firmly revealed the incident and come up with some robust programs to counter and detect surveillance on such hostile actors," explained Lokech.

Katumba Wamala was shot in the morning of Tuesday, June 1, at Kisota road, Kisaasi.

Accordingly to eyewitnesses, two armed assailants trailed the official's vehicle before opening fire.
