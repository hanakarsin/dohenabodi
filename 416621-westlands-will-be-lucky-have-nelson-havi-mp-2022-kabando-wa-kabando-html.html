Kabando Wa Kabando: Westlands Will Be Lucky to Have Nelson Havi as MP in 2022

  • Kabando's remarks came after Havis' speech during the National dialogue Conference on Electoral Justice in Kenya
  • During the conference, the LSK president said he is a member ANC party, and he will contest for the Westlands MP seat in 2022

Former Mukurweini MP Kabando Wa Kabando has said people of the Westland constituency will be lucky to have the president of Law Society of Kenya (LSK) Nelson Havi as their MP in 2022.

Taking to social media platforms, Kabando said Havi has consistently inspired many by his great moves and success in the political arena.

"You have been consistently inspiring, at least lately, if not always—vintage Havi delivering patriotic assault after another. Westlands will be lucky as their MP," he wrote.

Kabando's statement came after Havis' remark during the National dialogue Conference on Electoral Justice in Kenya.

During the conference, the LSK president also said he was a proud member of the Amani National Congress(ANC) party, adding that he will be contesting for the Westlands MP seat in 2022.

"I am proud to be associated with Amani National Congress party, which I am a member, and I am going to use it to vie for the MP seat in Westlands,""I have accomplished my three agendas in LSK by building a "new brave bar", and now I want to go and fix parliament."

However, as Havi eyes the Westlands MP seat, the current holder of the office, Tim Wanyonyi, seems to be doing an exemplary job in the area.

Recently, Wanyoyi was awarded the second-best performing MP in the country after Thika Town MP Patrick Wainaina by Mizani Africa.

Wanyonyi had 65.9%, while Wainaina topped with 65.9%.

A few days ago, Wanyonyi muted claims that he was eyeing the Nairobi city gubernatorial seat.

Responding to questions during an online session on Wednesday, June 2, Wanyonyi said he was focused on developing the Westlands constituency.

"No, I'm not going for the gubernatorial seat. And I'm not concerned with others vying for the seat - it's their democratic right to vie for any seat, including this one," he affirmed.

The second-time lawmaker who was once voted the best MP in Kenya has been issuing mixed signals regarding his 2022 political ambitions.
