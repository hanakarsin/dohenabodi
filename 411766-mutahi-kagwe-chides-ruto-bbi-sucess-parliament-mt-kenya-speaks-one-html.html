Mutahi Kagwe Scoffs at Ruto after BBI Success in Parliament: &quot;Mt Kenya Speaks as One&quot;

Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe has chided Deputy President William Ruto and his Tanga Tanga camp after lawmakers in the Senate and National Assembly overwhelmingly endorsed the 2020 Constitutional amendment Bill.

Speaking to Inooro TV on Wednesday, May 12, the former Nyeri senator said the success of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) in both Houses showed the vote-rich Mt Kenya region was behind President Uhuru Kenyatta.

"We were told that no one, not even a cat, would vote for the bill. So we went to Sagana and talked to the president and we exchanged views. They said the ward representatives had been bribed to pass it, so we said, let’s wait for the National Assembly and the Senate and now you see what happened,” he stated.

The politician argued that despite the political differences in Central Kenya, the region normally speaks in one voice when it matters.

Kagwe ridiculed the DP and his allies for boasting about numbers after dishing out a 'few billions in churches'.

“Did you say the BBI would fail because you were in a church where you gave KSh 2 billion? I have a lot of political experience and I will tell you this...Mt Kenya region speaks as one voice when it matters."

The CS claimed Ruto's meeting with a section of Mt Kenya leaders at Maasai Mara before the BBI vote failed terribly as some of them went against him during the hour of need.

BBI voting in parliament

The Senate passed the document on Tuesday, May 11, after 51 senators voted yes to 11 who shot it down.

Members of the National Assembly had earlier endorsed the bill by surpassing the 175 threshold that was needed.

Some of the Tanga Tanga MPs who voted yes included MPs Kimani Ngunjiri (Bahati), Charity Kathambi (Njoro) and Kareke Mbiuki (Maara).

While breaking his silence on the matter, an embattled Ruto, however, said the outcome of the vote was democratic and thus needed to be welcome by all despite the varied opinions.

Despite his reservations on the bill, the DP said the outcome ought to be respected, stating the electorate would also have the chance to decide on it.
