The head of state had revealed that he made Sonko the governor and initiated his impeachment over nonperformance, an allegation that the ex-governor vehemently refuted.

Following the breakdown of the duo's cordial relations, Sonko on Monday, January 18, took to Facebook to list his achievements as the Nairobi boss and the president's flaws.

The flamboyant politician in his onslaught to Uhuru claimed he had nothing to show despite borrowing over KSh 8 trillion in his eight years of administration; a statement which forms the basis of this piece.

"Despite borrowing over 8 trillion shillings in the last 8 years, what does the President have to show apart from unprecedented unemployment, poverty and hopelessness in millions of Kenyans?" Sonko posed.

KSh 5.4 trillion

TUKO.co.ke's fact check desk has established that the former Makadara MP inflated public debt as Kenya has not borrowed KSh 8 trillion under Uhuru's administration.

According to annual debt reports from the National Treasury, Kenya borrowed KSh4.8 trillion in the seven financial years from 2013/14 to 2019/20 as quoted by Africa Check.

The recent data available from the country’s Central Bank, ending November 2020, puts the figure at KSh5.46 trillion, still significantly short of Sonko’s claim.

Raising debt ceiling

Kenya’s public debt is not limited to the national conversation.

President Kenyatta's administration is now seeking to raise the country's debt ceiling from the current KSh 9 trillion to a new limit of KSh 12 trillion.

The move is aimed at enabling the government to borrow more in order to finance the 2021/2022 budget which, from the projections and all indications, is likely to suffer a serious financial shortfall running into billions of shillings due to revenue constraints.

The Treasury has since submitted a proposal to the Public Finance Management Regulation seeking approval to amend the regulations to pave way for more borrowing.

The Jubilee administration has defended the continued borrowing as investing for future benefit.
