Governor Anyang' Nyong'o deregisters all boda boda saccos over crime

- Governor Nyong'o Nyongo said there was clear indication the sector had been hijacked by criminals

- The county will formulate a new structure for the sector to organise and oversee election of the new association leaders

- At least 10 boda boda riders have been arrested in connection with the murder of two colleagues on April 11

Kisumu county has ordered deregistration of all boda boda organisations and freezing of their accounts following increased cases of crime.

In a press release, Governor Peter Anyang’ Nyong'o attributed recent senseless murders of two people in the county to criminals masquerading as Kisumu county Boda Boda Association members.

Nyongo further said there was clear indication the sector had been hijacked by external forces to carry out criminal activities in the area.

The governor revealed that in their joint county security committee meeting, they resolved to immediately deregister all the boda boda CBOs, saccos and associations within the county.

The county boss also directed that all banks accounts belonging to the associations be frozen until a new structure of the sector was organised in the region.

The county government would formulate a new structure for the sector to organise and oversee election of the new leaders in line with the County Transport Act.

The governor also ordered the county director of social development to communicate to all the bank the decision on suspending transaction of all boda boda CBOs.

At the same time, the governor warned all boda boda riders must operate within the traffic act and those found in the wrong should be arrested.

He also urged the police to expedite investigation into death of two riders who were allegedly attacked and killed by colleagues on April 11.

At least 10 boda boda riders have been arrested in connection with the incident.

Story by Naomi Akoth, TUKO.co.ke correspondent.
