Mwangi Wa Iria says COVID-19 measures barred him from attending Senate grilling

- Wa Iria was expected to appear before Senator Sam Ongeri-led committee to answer financial audit queries touching on county expenditure

- He said his county headquarters was declared a COVID-19 infection zone by the joint national and county government team

- He claimed it was in good faith that he did not want to interact with MPs some of whom were elderly and at a higher risk of contracting the virus

Murang'a governor Mwangi Wa Iria has blamed coronavirus situation in his county for his failure to honour The Senate Public Accounts and Investments Committee.

Wa Iria was expected to appear before Senator Sam Ongeri-led committee to answer financial audit queries touching on fiscal years 2015/2016, 2016/2017 and 2017/2018.

COVID-19 infection zone

In a letter addressed to the committee, Wa Iria said his county headquarters was declared a COVID-19 infection zone by the joint national and county government team.

All senior staff were put in mandatory self quarantine and isolation with expected results from Kenya Medical Research Institute yet to be released.

He claimed it was in good faith that he did not want to interact with MPs some of whom were elderly and at a higher risk of contracting the virus.

"It is totally against Public Health Act to deliberately and consciously put lives of senators in jeopardy and at risk of infection and contamination," he argued.

Arrest and fine

However, a visibly frustrated committee chairperson Sam Ongeri did not take the matter lightly and invoked Powers and Privileges Act to call for the politician's arrest.

“In view that the governor came under summons last year, and this year he has failed to honour summons, I order that this committee summons under police escort Governor Mwangi wa Iria to be produced by the IG physically to this committee room on September 10, 2020,” Ongeri said.

Ongeri also fined the governor KSh 500,000 for failing to honour the committee summons for the third time.

The law gives any parliamentary committee powers to impose fines, ranging from KSh200, 000 to KSh3 million.
