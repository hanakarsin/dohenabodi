CS Fred Matiang'i Threatens to Sue Dennis Itumbi for Linking Him to Ruaraka Land Grabbing

  • CS Fred Matiang’i wants Dennis Itumbi to apologise for referring to him as Ruaraka land grabber in series of his tweet
  • Matiang’i, through his lawyer, also asked Itumbi to accept the mistake and later engage him on the quantum of damages
  • The CS said in case Itumbi will face his demand, shall institute legal proceedings against him

Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang’i has threatened to sue Dennis Itumbi for linking him to the explosive Ruaraka land grabbing case.

Matiang’i's demand letter

The CS wrote a demand letter to Itumbi instructing him to retract a post on his Twitter account that referred to him as “the Ruaraka thief” or face legal action.

The letter written by V.A Nyamondi and Co Advocates directed the blogger to withdraw the tweet and give an unconditional apology for his actions.

“We are instructed to demand, as we hereby do, your immediate and unreserved retraction of all tweets referring to our client as a thief. Our client further demands an unconditional apology for the defamatory statements published via your verified Twitter account in a manner to be approved by our client,” the demand letter reads in part.

Matiang’i, through his lawyer, also asked Itumbi to accept the mistake and later engage him on the quantum of damages any other retribution for the defamatory publications.

“That if you do not comply with the terms of this demand by close of business Friday, August 13 2021, our client shall institute legal proceedings against you without any further reference to you and at your sole liability for all attendant costs and consequences,” the demand letter added.

The demand letter cited a series of tweets in which Itumbi referred to the interior CS as “Ruaraka Thief”.

The CS claimed that the tweets were defamatory, malicious and have ruined his reputation.

The interior CS maintained that the tweets have falsely been misunderstood by many, which resulted in him being found guilty of corruption which is not the case.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Itumbi had formally filed an application seeking to sue Interior CS Matiang'i over the KSh 1.5 billion Ruaraka land saga.

Itumbi appeared before the Milimani Anti-Corruption Court Chief Magistrate on Tuesday, November 24, 2020, to be granted leave to proceed with the case as a private prosecutor.
