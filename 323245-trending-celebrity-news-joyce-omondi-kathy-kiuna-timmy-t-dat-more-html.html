1. Stevo Simple Boy revelation on why he dropped out of school

A few months ago, Stevo Simple Boy alias Ndio Manake rose to the limelight after his song Vijana Tuache Mihadarati went viral on all social media platforms. He became a social media sensation and was featured in many local shows such as 10 Over 10.

However, few know about his education background. During a recent interview with Betty Kyallo, the singer who grew up in Homa Bay county opened up on how he used to sleep hungry and eventually dropped out of school.

Kupata karo ya kuenda shule ilikuwa ngumu sana; ma-time pia tulilala njaa, hakuna chakula tunapata. So ikipatakina tunakula, iki kosekana tunapenya tu hivyo. Niliachia class 8 juu karo haikuwa ya school fees saa ikabidi niachia hapo, said Stevo, disclosing that he is from a family of eight.

2. Kathy Kiuna narrates husband’s cancer battle

Jubilee Christian Church (JCC) reverend Kathy Kiuna, in the latest celeb news in Kenya came out to describe her husband’s battle with cancer.

Through her social media pages, the reverend disclosed how cancer treatment adverse effects rendered her husband, Allan Kiuna, weak.

Last year, it was a difficult time for us. My husband @bishopkiuna was diagnosed with cancer and was away seeking treatment. Chemotherapy had him losing hair, gaining weight, and unable to do much physically; he was unable to stand or walk for long and had to tiptoe around from the immense pain in his legs,” Kathy said, adding, “Chemotherapy had him at his lowest. What was most difficult for me was having to be home preaching most of the time and not by his side physically, and honestly speaking, it took all of me to even stand and preach.

3. Willis Raburu responds to pressure to have children from fans

Citizen TV presenter and media personality Willis Raburu and his love of life Mary, in the latest Kenya celeb news, are not bowing down to pressure by fans to have children.

Speaking in an interview, Willis talked about how he and his love deal with the ever-growing pressure and constant reminder from fans.

Raburu gave credit to Mary, his wife, for teaching him to be patient and not to heed to dispensable pressure.

One of the greatest things my wife has taught me is to be happy at whatever state we are in. So we don’t really pay attention to pressures. However, I will say this, society does put a lot of pressure on people to bring forth children, and it's okay, but we all have to be sensitive to people's feelings because you never know what they have been through or are going through. We must, therefore, articulate our desires with a dose of sensitivity, he stated.

Gengetone artists in the Ethic Crew addressed their fans regarding the controversies surrounding their latest song.

The song video, which was premier on 1st November became the centre of social media bashing with a lot of guardians and netizens calling out for its withdrawal.

On their Twitter account, Ethics Crew admitted that they respected women and that their song Tarimbo was not aimed at promoting what many termed as sexual assault culture.

As artists, we are sons to our mothers, brothers to our sisters, uncles to our nieces and friends to our female friends and fans, and it is not in any way our intention to publicize violence or [sexual assault] against these highly revered individuals in our community, Ethic Crew tweeted.

Alikiri kwamba ni kosa amefanya, lakini alisema kwamba hakujua kwamba ni kosa ama alipaswa kutuma video kwanza,umuelimisha kwanza kiutaratibu kwamba video yeyote inapaswa kupitia kwenye hii bodi… Kwa hivyo akakiri kwamba ameelewa utaratibu huo ambao alikua haujui, executive secretary of TFB, Dr. Kiagho Kilonzo, told Bongo 5.

7. Fena Gitu rants about how fellow Kenyan artists don’t support each other

Famous Kenyan songstress Fena Gitu went ham on all the Kenyan musicians for snubbing her album listening party.

Speaking through her Twitter account, the singer revealed that only a handful of Kenyan musicians showed up on the event despite inviting most of them.

Kenyan artistes barely show up for fellow artistes. There were like four artistes (one who is my best friend, so it doesn’t count) at my listening party, and a handful last night Elani Muziki’s listening party. And we want our fans to support us. Anyway, the album is for fans, she tweeted.

Her complaint comes just a few weeks after Gospel-secular musician Willy Paul called out on the music cartels in the country. According to him, the said cartels determines which artists are invited to primary functions and also the ones who get awards.

…. f***k all the industry cartels, look at all the love in this video!! Ever wondered why some of your favourite artists are not in some events that’s you’d expect them to be in? It’s because in Kenya if don’t dance to a cartel’s tune then you are an enemy. If you act like you know your worth you’re an enemy, if you keep your brand high you’re an enemy, if you release hits you’re an enemy, if they call you for a deal, and you don’t give them a cut you are an enemy, and they’ll end up giving that space to someone who doesn’t deserve it... this is so sad... yaani it’s about who knows who!!! what’s happening to our industry???” The Saldido Boss wrote.
