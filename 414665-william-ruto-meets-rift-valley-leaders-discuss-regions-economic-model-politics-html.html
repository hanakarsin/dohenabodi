William Ruto Meets Rift Valley Leaders to Discuss Region's Economic Model, Politics

- Deputy William Ruto, on Monday, June 7, met political leaders from his region to talk about the current economic state of the region

- This was after the leaders from the Rift Valley felt left out following the recent tour and launch of big projects by President Uhuru Kenyatta in the Nyanza region

- The frosty relationship between Uhuru and Ruto has intensified feelings of dissatisfaction among leaders from the Rift Valley where the DP hails from

Deputy President William Ruto has met leaders from the Rift Valley region to discuss the region's political and economic fortunes.

This comes a few days after President Uhuru Kenyatta toured the Nyanza region and launched multi-billion projects, making leaders from Rift Valley feel excluded from the Jubilee administration's development plans.

A section of the leaders feels like the president has sidelined the region in terms of development projects, the same feelings other regions have harboured against the central region, which has produced three presidents.

It is obvious a meeting like this is not about who will succeed Uhuru in 2022 because it is believed the region already has their choice, which is Ruto.

Baringo senator Gideon Moi, who is from the vote-rich region, has also expressed interest in the seat.

It is also not the first time the DP is holding a meeting with his allies to discuss matters to do with his highly publicised bottom-up economic model.

Recently, Ruto held a meeting with his close associates from the Mt. Kenya region and the coastal region.

During the meetings, it was evident Ruto is on a serious hunt for a person who will run with him in 2022 and if successfully elected in office by voters, land the deputy president position.

The second-in-command has also met leaders from Coast Kenya alongside economist David Ndii and talked about the hustler narrative and the bottom-up economic model.
