Who is the best dancer in the world? Top 20 most famous dancers

Even though dance is part of all cultures, some individuals have taken the craft to the next level, thus earning themselves international recognition. There are probably millions of dancers globally, but who is the best dancer in the world?

To many, dance is a hobby, but to others, it is not only a profession but also a way of life. The question regarding the best dancer in the world is best answered by going back in time and comparing dancers across different generations. Indeed, dance has greatly evolved, but it is not surprising to see people who died decades ago still ranked ahead of today’s dancers.

1. Michael Jackson

Michael is the most popular dancer in the world. At the prime of his career, Michael Jackson was a crowd mover who had fans flock stadiums to watch him do the iconic moonwalk. He rose quickly to become one of the most popular pop stars in the 1980s. MJ is said to have had an amazing talent for dance and rhythm from a young age.

The star looked as energetic on the dance floor in his 50s. Even at his death, his place as the best dancer in the world of all time cannot be disputed.

2. Mikhail Baryshnikov

Mikhail Baryshnikov is one of the best male dancers. The Russian American dancer and choreographer was born in the Soviet era. He grew to be one of the best male ballet dancers in the world. At one point, he was the artistic director in the American ballet theatre and the New York City ballet.

7. Martha Graham

True dance lovers know the story of Martha Graham because she single-handedly reshaped American dance. Not so many people have dance styles named after them, but Martha Graham is among the lucky ones. The Graham technique (created by Martha) has been labelled as the cornerstone of American modern dance.

It has been taught for close to a century and continues to influence other modern techniques. Considering all that, she can be described as one of the best female dancers in the world.

8. Usher

American singer, songwriter, actor, entrepreneur and dancer Usher Raymond grew up in an era when music videos were equally as important as songs. This meant he had to spice up his music videos with unique dance moves.

His style of dance was recognized, earning him a spot among the most talented dancers in the world. He may not be the best breakdancer in the world ever, but he ranks up there among the best.

9. Prabhu Deva

Prabhu Deva is not an ordinary dancer. The Indian national is also an actor, choreographer, and producer of Indian dance. He has had a long, illustrious career of more than 25 years.

He is credited for introducing international dancing styles in Indian movies through his work in Tamil, Telugu, Bollywood, Malayalam, and Kannada films. Michael Jackson is his idol and dance influence. As a result, Prabhu has perfected styles such as the iconic moonwalk, hip-thrust and quirky moves.

10. Rudolf Nureyev

Nureyev had already established himself as one of the best dancers in the world before he died in 1993. This is even though he started his career late but compensated for that by pushing himself hard rehearsing for hours. He was laid to rest in a Russian cemetery in Sainte-Genevieve-des-Bois near Paris more than two decades ago.

11. Les Twins

Les Twins is a hip hop duo from Paris, France. Their names are Larry and Laurent Bourgeois. They are recognized internationally for their talents in new styles of hip-hop dancing and other dancing styles. The twin brothers have won multiple awards for dancing together, including the 2017 World of Dance competition.

They debuted their career dancing on the streets of Paris for passersby. The biggest break in their career came in 2008 when they became the finalists in Paris' Incroyable Talent show. Over the course of their career, they have performed with renowned celebrities, including Beyonce, Jay-Z, and Missy Elliot. The twins are the founding members of the Criminal Crew and Eleven Paris.

20. Sofia Boutella

Boutella was born in Algeria, where she started studying ballet at the age of five. She relocated to France with her parents when she was 10. In France, she took refuge in dance studios. She quickly added rhythmic gymnastics to her repertoire, competing on France's national team at the age of 16.

She was introduced to hip-hop and soon found herself on stage dancing for famous artists, including Michael Jackson and Madonna. She is famous for the hip-hop and street dance, known as the Nike Girl from Nike Women advertising campaigns.
