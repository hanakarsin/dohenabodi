Kenya Power MD Bernard Ngugi Resigns, Replaced by Rosemary Oduor in Acting Capacity

Kenya Power Board of Directors has announced the appointment of Rosemary Oduor as the acting managing director and the chief executive officer.

The appointment followed the surprise resignation of Bernard Ngugi who has been at the helm of the lighting company since October 2019.

Ngugi replaced the former acting managing director Jared Othieno who served from July 2018 after the suspension of Kenya Power's management team that was led by Ken Tarus.

The board's chairperson Vivienne Yeda said the change of leadership takes effect immediately from Wednesday, August 4.

Oduor is a holder of a Bachelor of Technology Degree in Electrical and Communication Engineering from Moi University and a Master of Business Administration from the University of Nairobi.

The incoming managing director joined the company in 1991 and served various positions before rising to the position of general manager in charge of commercial services and sales.

In March 2021, Ngugi announced new plans to reduce power bills for consumers after a public uproar.

Ngugi said the company was seeking to lower fixed charges in contracts signed with electricity-generating companies such as KenGen and pass the benefits to its customers.

In the plans, the public liability company wants to cut the costs spent on generators that have been used for the thermal production of electricity.

Ngugi noted cheap power would improve the dwindling economic fortunes in the country considering many small and large scale companies depend on electricity for day to day activities.

Once KPLC completes negotiations with electricity producers such as Gulf Power, Iberafrica, Tsavo Power, OrPower, Lake Turkana Wind and Thika Power, it would review prices downwards.

Kenya Power currently charges most domestic customers between KSh 16 and KSh 24 per kilowatt-hour (kWh) depending on how they consume it.
