Jakoyo Midiwo: Ex-MP Was in Nairobi to Celebrate Daughter's Graduation When He Fell Sick, Died

  • Former Gem MP Jakoyo Midiwo passed away on Monday, June 14, at around 4pm
  • His elder brother George Midiwo said the politician had limited his trips to Nairobi
  • He only travelled to the capital to celebrate his children's exemplary performance in their academics
  • His son had scored an A in KCSE 2020 while his daughter had graduated from a South African university

Former Gem MP Jakoyo Midiwo was in Nairobi to celebrate his children's exemplary performance in their academics when he fell sick and later died, TUKO.co.ke has learnt.

The vocal politician died on the evening of Monday, June 14, while receiving treatment at the Nairobi Hospital after suffering a cardiac arrest.

According to a report by the Standard, the ODM politician had limited his visits to Nairobi but was forced to travel to the city to celebrate his son's KCSE performance as he had scored an A while his daughter had just graduated from a South African university.

Low blood pressure

On Saturday, June 12, the former lawmaker fell ill and was rushed to the Nairobi Hospital for treatment. Doctors would later find out he has low blood pressure and he was thus moved to the intensive care unit.

"The doctors had earlier informed us he was diagnosed with acute kidney failure, and they were trying to establish its cause,” the deceased's elder brother, George said.

He passed on at 4pm on Monday and a post-mortem is scheduled to determine the exact cause of death.

As TUKO.co.ke reported, the ODM party confirmed the news of Midiwo's demise on Monday and eulogised him as a gallant son of the soil and a defender of the party who never shied away from speaking the truth.

"He was a defender of the party and never shied away from speaking the truth. To the family, we say pole sana," the statement from ODM indicated.

The former lawmaker passed away just days after his sister's death who is set to be buried on Saturday, June 19.

Politicians and Kenyans at large took to their social media platforms to mourn the departed leader who served as the Gem MP from 2002 to 2007 under Narc and 2013 to 2017 under ODM.
