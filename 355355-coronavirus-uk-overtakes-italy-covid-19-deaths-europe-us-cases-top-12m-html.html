Coronavirus: UK overtakes Italy in COVID-19 deaths as US cases top 1.2M

- Globally, over 3.6 million cases of coronavirus had been confirmed with over 253,000 deaths in 212 countries and territories as of Tuesday, May 5

- This came as countries rushed to develop vaccines against the pandemic while others such as Madagascar turned to herbal medicine

- Kenya with 535 confirmed cases and 24 deaths was one of the two countries in Africa that were cleared by WHO to participate in COVID-19 drug trials

The United Kingdom (UK) has surpassed Italy as the country with the highest number of COVID-19 deaths in Europe.

According to DW News, the UK had registered 32,000 deaths as of Tuesday, May 5, more than any other country in Europe.

Italy, which was the epicentre of the pandemic in Europe, had recorded over 29,000 deaths while Spain was third with at least 25,600 deaths followed by France (25,200) and Germany (6,900).

Globally, over 3.6 million cases of the virus had been confirmed with over 253,000 deaths in 212 countries and territories and two international conveyances.

In this, the United States (US) led in both the number of infections ( 1.2 million) and death toll (70,000).

The cases came as countries rushed to develop vaccines against the virus while others turned to herbal medicine.

Kenya with 535 confirmed cases and 24 deaths was one of the two countries in Africa that were cleared by WHO to participate in COVID-19 drug trials.

This was despite President Uhuru Kenyatta saying Kenyans would not be used as guinea pigs.

"Our institutions like KEMRI, and the Kenya Primate Centre are working with other teams to seek vaccine for this disease. When they tell us they have developed vaccines to a point that it will require human testing we will tell you," "They are involved in global effort and I assure you nothing will be done in the dark, we will tell you. So don’t cause fear and panic to fellow Kenyans ," Uhuru said on Saturday, April 25.

The vaccine study dubbed solidarity trial would be conducted in 70 countries.

Others spread out in Asia and Europe were Germany, Honduras, India, Indonesia, Iran, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Lebanon, Malaysia, Norway, Peru, Philippines, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Spain, Switzerland and Thailand.

The WHO selected three treatment options namely remdesivir, lopinavir/ritonavir, opinavir/ritonavir with interferon beta-1a and chloroquine or hydroxychloroquine.
