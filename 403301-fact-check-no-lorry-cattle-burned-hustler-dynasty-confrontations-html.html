Fact check: No lorry with cattle was burned in hustler versus dynasty confrontations

- A Facebook post claimed that the lorry was set alight by angry boda boda riders in Nakuru after a freak accident with one of them

- The photo was circulated by various individuals who called out Deputy President William Ruto for allegedly fueling tribal wars

- A reverse image search has, however, revealed that the incident happened in Nigeria

A distressing photo of cows burnt in a truck made rounds on social media claiming the vehicle was torched as a result of the hustler versus dynasty confrontations.

The photo was circulated by various individuals, majorly the political class and activists who called out Deputy President William Ruto for allegedly fueling tribal wars through his hustler movement.

A Facebook post which was first made on January 14, 2021, claimed that the lorry was set alight by angry boda boda riders in Nakuru after a freak accident with one of them.

“When Boda Boda riders descended on this lorry in Nakuru after a freak accident with one of them, they were still heard shouting ‘Dynasty! Choma!’” one post reads. “They burnt the lorry together with the live cows that were being transported in it," read the post. "The poisonous hustler vs dynasty narrative is not about politics anymore, it's about your neighbor thinking that you've made it in life and destroying/robbing all you've worked for,” the post further stated.

Even though there have been reports of boda boda riders burning vehicles believed to be owned by "dynasties", but did the hustler versus dynasty 'war' lead to the torching of a lorry with cattle inside?

It happened in Nigeria

A reverse image search done by the AfricaCheck revealed that the incident happened in Nigeria and was first published on Facebook on January 12, 2021.

This came two days before the image appeared in Kenya.

According to Nation Online, a Nigerian outlet, the area police spokesperson Olugbenga Fadeyi confirmed the incident adding that the boy was rushed to the hospital where he died.

TUKO.co.ke's fact check desk also established that more images of the incident had been published by Nigerian websites.

Therefore, the image had nothing to do with Kenya's hustler versus dynasty confrontations.
