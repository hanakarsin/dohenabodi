Ndindi Nyoro: Kenyans Remind Kiharu MP How He Asked Uhuru to Rule With Iron Fist

  • On Monday, August 2, Deputy President William Ruto was barred from travelling to Uganda due to an alleged lack of clearance from authorities
  • His allies decried the happening, claiming the deputy president was being humiliated, adding that Kenya was slowly being reverted to a dictatorship
  • However, a section of Kenyans was keen to remind the politicians of how they had cheered the state when it meted ruthlessness on the opposition

A section of Kenyans has revisited some of the sentiments previously made by politicians who condoned oppressive governance but now appear to be victims of the autocratic regime they once cheered.

A few instances

Following the nullification of the presidential results in 2017 by the Supreme Court, the politicians, who are now allied to Deputy President William Ruto, were on record calling on the government to sideline the Judiciary, claiming the opposition was leveraging on the arm of government to acquire power.

Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro had advised President Uhuru Kenyatta to rule by an iron fist and suspend the constitution should the apex court nullify the results in the October 26, 2017 repeat polls.

After Uhuru's swearing, unrest was experienced in the country, the politicians again urging the state to deal with the vindictive NASA supporters who, led by their leaders, had vowed to trouble Uhuru's second term.

During Miguna Miguna's scuffle at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA), the politicians and their cronies once again urged the state to shun clemency while handling the lawyer's matter.

Miguna had been faulted for unlawfully administering an oath to ODM leader Raila Odinga. He was later deported to Canada.

Ruto had dismissed Fred Matiang'i's detractors saying the Interior CS was doing his work.

A few years into the above instances, the leaders who cheered then have now found themselves on the receiving end of the "state humiliation".

This came into perspective on Monday, August 2, when Ruto was barred from flying to Uganda due to an alleged lack of clearance from relevant authorities.

Ruto's allies cry foul

His loyalists claimed he was being disrespected and humiliated, adding that the state was keen on sabotaging his 2022 presidential.

Taking to social media, the politicians said that by Ruto's flight being obstructed, Kenya was being turned into an aristocratic nation.

"I have always been telling you that we are officially back to the dark days of KANU and KADU. The so called Deep State wanatufinya hatupumui," Oscar Sudi said."When The Time Is Right, I The Lord Will Make It Happen…. Hii madharau ni ya muda, itaisha," said Millicent Omanga."We ALL know Matiang’i and Kinyua would NEVER out of their own accord require the DP to have clearance to travel. That is simply ABOVE their pay grade. Someone is hiding behind them. 12 Months to go and it’s good riddance," Susan Kihika said.

However, a section of netizens was keen to remind the politicians of what they had previously advocated for, now taking a toll on them.

"In 2017 when Muguna Miguna was kicked out of the country, Itumbi said: in this the police did well, Murkomen: It is a small issue, Ndindi Nyoro: We should rethink democracy, it is not serving us well. We need a benevolent dictator, Williams Ruto: Let the government agencies do their work."

@Kamoh James:

"Ndindi Nyoro lamentations are like those of a young girl who has just started seeing her menses. They cheered the government way back in 2017 and enacted policies to punish the opposition. The drama is yet to start , they should be patient."
