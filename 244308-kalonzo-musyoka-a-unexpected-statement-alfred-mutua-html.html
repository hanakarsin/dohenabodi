Kalonzo Musyoka makes a rather unexpected, mean statement about Alfred Mutua

- Kalonzo Musyoka has taunted Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua after it emerged that a Mutua supporter was hounded out of a passenger bus by women

- In a video that has been circulating online, a man was reprimanded inside a passenger bus after claiming that Alfred Mutua deserved another term in office

- Kalonzo speaking in Nairobi on Wednesday, June 21 said that there was nothing wrong in heckling a Mutua supporter

- Kalonzo had joined Wavinya Ndeti who has since been cleared to contest for the Machakos gubernatorial seat on a Wiper ticket

While celebrating Wavinya Ndeti's triumph over IEBC, Kalonzo seized the opportunity to throw shade at Alfred Mutua.

He alluded to the recent video in which women from Machakos had bundled a man from passenger bus after he asked them to vote for Alfred Mutua in August.

Speaking in Nairobi, Kalonzo heartily said that there was nothing wrong in Governor Mutua being heckled.

"Mimi nimestaajabu kuona ya kuwa akina mama wanafukuza watu kwa matatu... watu wameerevuka siku hizi (I was shocked that women chased people from a matatu. People have become intelligent," Kalonzo said.

Kalonzo added that the county government had stolen public funds and almost brought the county to its feet.

"Let us clean the rot in Machakos county and start with those who have stolen from the public coffers, lets do a clean up by electing Wavinya," Kalonzo added.

In the said video, the women had attacked the man calling him a traitor who did not deserve to be in the same bus with them,

Ndeti's supporters pointed out Mutua's failures and having let down the people of Machakos since being elected in 2013.

They sought to make it clear that the Kamba nation is deep inside NASA and needs a different person as governor to replace Alfred Mutua.

Wavinya Ndeti’s gubernatorial ambitions received a major boost after the High court quashed a ruling by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission disputes committee ruling.

The Wiper gubernatorial candidate had been put on the spot after a petition was filed alleging that she was a member of two parties, which would have consequently rendered her ineligible to contest for an elective seat.
