Dennis Itumbi arrested over letter linked to DP Ruto's alleged assassination plot

- Dennis Itumbi was picked up from a Nairobi street by Flying Squad officers

- The digital strategist at Ruto's office is under probe to establish source of the letter

- The viral letter detailed a meeting at Nairobi's high-end La Mada Hotel

- At least four CSs attended the said meeting to allegedly plan how to eliminate Ruto

- The mentioned CSs confirmed they attended the meeting but vehemently denied knowledge of the letter

Dennis Itumbi, secretary digital, innovations and diaspora communications at the Office of the President has been picked up by detectives over letter linked to Deputy President William Ruto’s alleged assassination plot.

The digital communication strategist who operates from Ruto's Harambee Annex office was arrested by Flying Squad officers seeking to establish source of the controversial letter.

The officers pounced on him at a Nairobi street at around 1:30pm on Wednesday, July 3, hurriedly bundled him onto a standby car with a civilian number plate then sped off.

It was not immediately clear where the detectives were taking Itumbi but multiple sources indicated he was driven to the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) headquarters for questioning.

Sources privy to the ongoing probe had zeroed in on two staff at Ruto's office whom they suspected may have generated the viral letter.

The letter alleged a meeting was held at La Mada Hotel in Nairobi to plan how to stop Ruto from ascending to power in 2022.

The meeting was reportedly attended by CSs James Macharia (Transport), Joseph Mucheru (ICT), Sicily Kariuki (Health) and Peter Munya (Trade).

Although the CSs confirmed they attended the meeting, Munya, on behalf of his colleagues, vehemently denied the allegations against them.
