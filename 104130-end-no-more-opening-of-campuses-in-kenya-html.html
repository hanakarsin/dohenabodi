Government bans opening of more satellite campuses

- Many satellite campuses in Kenya are said to have watered down the quality of higher education in the country

- Commission for University Education (CUE) closed 10 campuses belonging to Kisii University a month ago over the same

- Education CS Fred Matiang'i has now ordered a stop to further registration of satellite campuses in a bid to save the qaulity of higher education in Kenya

READ ALSO: LIST: 10 Kisii University Campuses To Be Closed - CUE

The government has suspended further opening of more satellite campuses with immediate effect in a bid to end the rampant mushrooming of the institutions which are believed to have watered down the quality of higher education in Kenya.

Speaking on Tuesday, March 8, Education, Science and Technology Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang’i said that the move is aimed at improving the quality of education offered in higher institutions of learning to avoid producing incompetent professionals to the job market.

The CS has urged authorities responsible to stop further registration of such campuses, saying they are offering poor quality education to the masses.

According to the CS, local universities have also strayed from their core function by offering courses that are not their strength.

This, Matiang’i says, has led to the production of poor human resource, resulting to detrimental production in various fields.

The latest comes as the number of sprouting satellite campus keeps hitting the rooftops each day.

Kisii University was among the institutions whose 10 campuses were shut down a month ago over poor quality education.

The Commission for University Education (CUE) ordered the closure of the 10 campuses within 90 days as they failed to meet outlined thresholds.

The campuses affected were: Eldoret, Eldama Ravine, Nyamira, Kabarnet, Migori, Ogembo, Keroka, Kehancha, Kapenguria and Isebania.

A number of other campuses were also under review and had been given 90 days to address various aspects raised by the commission’s inspectors.

The campuses, as reported by the paper, were: Jaramogi Oginga Odinga University of Science and Technology (Kisumu); Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (Nakuru); Kisii University campuses in Kisumu, Kericho and Kitale; Laikipia University campuses in Nakuru, Eldoret and Naivasha; Moi University campuses in Kitale and Odera Akang’o; University of Eastern Africa-Baraton (Eldoret campus); University of Eldoret (Eldoret CBD) and University of Kabianga (Kapkatet campus).
