MP David Sankok advises 19-year-old daughter to stay away from men with fake identities

National Council of Persons with Disability Chairman, David Ole Sankok has ignited the waterworks yet again after advising his daughter to stay away from ‘fake’ men.

The proud and dedicated dad asked his girl to find herself a genuine man when she feels ready to settle down for marriage.

In a sweet Facebook message to celebrate his girls 19th birthday, Sankok said he will support his princess with the choices she makes in life.

However, the loving father asked his girl to stay away men who tend to be swayed like a flag by the wind.

To the Member of Parliament (MP), some men change their accents, copy styles or even make their women apply makeup just to look like the ladies in movies.

“My daughter, get someone who lives his life without copying others because they may end up copying devil's agents. Again, you may love a photocopy of a man and when their true image comes out it may be too late,” he said.

The politician asked his girl to run away if she realizes a man is not being true to himself or seems like a con.

“I am not a prophet to know and I will not pretend to have spiritual eyes to differentiate good or bad wealth. I have concentrated on character because good values are everything I need from you and your husband to be,” he continued.

The doting dad described his girl as the greatest gift he has ever received.

Earlier, the proud dad gifted his daughter with a car after she performed exemplarily in her Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examinations.

An emotional Sankok penned a sweet message to celebrate his girls win as he proudly said she is a lady to be admired.
