A Tanzanian Member of Parliament has expressed shock after learning that the salaries and allowances they take home are lower than those their colleagues in Kenya earn.

According to The Citizen newspaper, Mbogwe MP Nicodamus Maganga said that he did not know that allowances paid to legislators were not enough before he was elected to Parliament.

“Before I was elected an MP, I thought allowances were more than enough. However, when you look at other parliaments such as those in Kenya and South Africa, our colleagues there are paid in dollars unlike us here at home,” Maganga said.

Kenyan lawmakers monthly salaries

According to the Business Daily, each MP in Kenya earns a basic KSh 710,000, meaning their employer, the Parliamentary Service Commission(PSC), currently spends a minimum KSh 3.54 billion annually KSh 295.36 million monthly on their salaries.

The bicameral Parliament has 349 members in the National Assembly and 67 in the Senate, excluding the Speakers.

Should the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) proposal be ratified by Kenyans, the PSC will require an additional KSh 323.76 million to meet the basic pay of MPs annually or Sh26.98 million monthly.

The lawmakers are also entitled to juicy sitting and travel perks, mileage allowance, KSh 250,000 housing allowance, KSh 5 million car grant, KSh 7 million car loan and KSh 20 million mortgages.
