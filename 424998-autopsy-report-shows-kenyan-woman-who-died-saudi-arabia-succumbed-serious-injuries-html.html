Autopsy Report Shows Kenyan Woman Who Died in Saudi Arabia Succumbed to Serious Injuries

  • Until her death which was reported on June 2, Maximilla Muhandia was working in the gulf as a house help
  • Her family said a week before they received the news of her demise, Muhandia had informed them that she was in danger
  • A post-mortem revealed the mother of two was hit with a blunt object on her head and was tortured before death

An autopsy report has shown that a Kenyan woman who died in Saudi Arabia succumbed to serious injuries which were inflicted on her using a blunt object.

Maximilla Muhandia was working in the gulf until her death which was reported on June 2, 2021, and her family received her body a week ago after more than three months of trying to access it from Saudi.

According to a report by the Standard, Muhandia had serious injuries on her head and the post-mortem also concluded that she was tortured before bleeding to death.

"My daughter was tortured. She was hit on the head with a blunt object in an attack that distorted her face. We have been told she bled to death," the deceased's mother said during her burial in Nandi on Thursday, September 2.

The family said a week before they received the news of her death, their daughter made distressful calls home saying she was receiving death threats.

Muhandia's kin reckoned that if the Kenyan embassy took action when she raised alarm over the danger that she was in, her death could have been avoided.

The family is now asking the government to launch a probe into their Muhandia's death saying her employer is the main suspect as the mother of two died while working for her.
