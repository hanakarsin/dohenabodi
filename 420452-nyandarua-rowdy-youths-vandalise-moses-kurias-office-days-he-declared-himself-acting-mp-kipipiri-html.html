Nyandarua: Rowdy Youths Vandalise Moses Kuria's Office Days after He Declared Himself Acting MP for

  • The youths were protesting alleged disparaging remarks made against the leaders in the county, including Governor Francis Kimemia
  • They burnt Moses Kuria's portraits as they vandalised his party's office by painting it red
  • The local leaders called on Deputy President William Ruto, urging him to tame his irritating loyalists

Chama Cha Kazi offices opened in Nyandarua by Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria were on Wednesday, July 28, vandalised by youths protesting alleged derogatory utterances made by the MP against Governor Francis Kimemia.

TUKO.co.ke understands Kuria lost the stewardship of the People Empowerment Party (PEP) and thus embarked on forming and popularising the CCK party.

While attending a church service in the county on July 25, Kuria, alongside his Kikuyu counterpart Kimani Ichungw'a, is said to have ridiculed the local leaders, including the governor, using words that seem to have offended the locals.

The youths, in a fury, graffitied the recently opened offices as they burnt portraits belonging to the Gatundu South MP.

"We don't want him in our county. He should go open the offices in Gatundu. He should leave our governor alone. Our county is growing with him (Kimemia)," the youths chanted.

The locals, through their leaders, accused the outspoken legislator of disrespect, calling on Deputy President William Ruto to help tame his foot soldiers.

"We are disgusted by the utterances by Kuria and Ichung'wa. The utterances divides our county. No one will respect our governor. I want to urge other leaders to stop fighting our leaders when they come to Nyandarua," one of the local leaders said."William Ruto must them. He always carries with him the Bible. When Kuria abused our governor we saw him (Ruto) laughing. We condenm the utterances of Kuria and Ichung'wa. Their leader should teach them christianity," added another.

Acting Kipiri MP

While in Nyandarua, Kuria overlooked the leadership of Kipipiri MP Amos Kimunya, saying the majority leader in the National Assembly had failed in his duties.

Kuria declared himself the acting area MP until when the next polls are held.

While boasting of the Hustler Nation's successes in the recent by-elections, he said he felt bad for the people of Kipipiri for having Kimunya as their MP.

"When I go to church, I pray for all of you because you do not have a good MP. So today, for the remaining year, I declare myself the acting Kipipiri MP," he said.
