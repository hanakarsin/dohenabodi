Chris Kirubi: Kenyan Celebrities Mourn Tycoon with Glowing Tributes, Thank Him for Being Supportive

  • Chris Kirubi was loved by many creatives for supporting their work and he played a major role in the lives of some of the top celebrities
  • Capital FM presenter Miano Muchiri celebrated Kirubi, the Capital Group chairman, in a heartwarming message, thanking him for giving him a job on the radio
  • Musician King Kanja reminisced the first time he met the tycoon when he was 16 years old and how they became great friends
  • Mourning Kirubi, DJ Joe Mfalme wrote: "My first time to the US was made possible because he paid for my flight. Thank you for everything"

Kenya has lost a great businessman, entrepreneur and industrialist, Chris Kirubi.

The business mogul, popularly referred to as DJ CK, passed away on Monday, July 14.

Many among them celebrities, especially from the music industry, have paid glowing tributes to Kirubi. They have described him as a mentor, very supportive, a man who believed in talent and gave them opportunities.

Legendary singer Nameless revealed that Kirubi played an important role in his marriage to Wahu. In a touching tribute, the father of two disclosed that they were planning to feature the tycoon on their docu-reality series.

"This is sad. Chris was a good friend to me and Wahu. We met him those days when Wahu was modelling for Miss Kenya and he joked about it all the time till late last year. We had planned to call him to do an interview for our docu-reality because he was an important part of our journey . This news has shocked me. Rest well dear friend! You will forever inspire many with your life story," Nameles wrote.

Below are more tributes from Showbiz bigwigs.

Size 8's husband DJ Mo thanked the fallen tycoon for hiring him at Capital FM.

"Met him in 2010 and he believed in me and my career. He held my hand, called me in his office and that’s how I landed my show on capital FM. He used to guide me on so many things , I learnt a lot from him when it came to business. Rest In Peace my boss," DJ posted.

Kirubi supported Nonini's music career, and he even featured in the king of Genge's Kataa Hio hit song, which was released back in 2009.

Capital presenter Miano Muchiri, celebrated Kirubi, the Capital Group Chairman, in a heartwarming message, thanking him for giving him a job on radio.

"Doctor, DJ, CK, thank you for the endless opportunities you gave not only me but to everyone that worked for you. You gave me a shot at radio @capitalfmkenya because you believed in me. You guided my career for seven years, I will forever be grateful. I say this with a heavy heart. Rest In Peace Chairman," read Miano's message.

Musician King Kanja reminisced the first time he met the tycoon when he was 16 years old and became great friends.

Celebrated disc jockey Joe Mfalme also penned a touching tribute to Kirubi.

To a man that I have admired and someone who gave so many of us opportunities. I have nothing but great and fond memories...My career kicked off at his station and his leadership and guidance has impacted so many of us in the media industry. My first time to the States was made possible because he paid for my flight. Thank you for everything. To a great boss, leader and legend, rest well," he said.

Last message

Hours before his demise, the 80-year-old, as his routine, took to Twitter to share a motivational quote where he wished his followers success, achievements and happiness.

"Top of the morning to you, my dear friend. It doesn’t have to be a tiring Monday. Instead, I bring to you good tidings of favour, success, achievements and happiness in the new week. Give yourself a go towards your success plan. Enjoy today and the rest of your week," Kirubi said.
