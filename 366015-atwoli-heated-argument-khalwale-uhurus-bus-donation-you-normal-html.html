Atwoli in heated argument with Khalwale over Uhurus bus donation: &quot;You are not normal&quot;

- Boni Khalwale slammed Western Kenya leaders for visiting President Uhuru Kenyatta only to be given a bus donation

- According to the former legislator, the leaders could have asked for better things like an increase in budgetary allocations for Western Kenya counties

- Francis Atwoli responded to the ex-senator saying it was inappropriate for him to conclude that way

The Central Organisation of Trade Unions (COTU) Secretary-General, Francis Atwoli, has been engaged in a heated exchange with former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale over President Uhuru Kenyatta's bus donation to Nzoia Sugar Football Club.

It all started when Khalwale slammed Western Kenya leaders for meeting the president only to be given a bus.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the head of state met the leaders at Harambee House in Nairobi on Wednesday, July 1, where he handed over a brand new 52-seater bus to Nzoia Sugar Company chairman Joash Wamang'oli.

Leaders who attended the meeting were governors Wycliffe Oparanya (Kakamega), Sospeter Ojaamong (Busia), Wycliffe Wangamati (Bungoma), Wilber Ottichilo (Vihiga) and Patrick Khaemba (Trans-Nzoia).

Others were Devolution CS Eugene Wamalwa and COTU boss Francis Atwoli.

According to Khalwale, the leaders ought to have asked for better things like an increase in budgetary allocation for Busia, Kakamega, Bungoma, Trans Nzoia and Vihiga counties which were all allocated KSh 40 billion in the 2019/2020 Financial Year.

"Despite the counties of Busia, Kakamega, Bungoma Vihiga and Trans Nzoia receiving a combined allocation of over KSh 40 billion in 2019/20, yesterday, our county heads went to the president to request for a bus! Inconceivable! Lucky Uhuru didn't do a Sonko on them," said Khalwale.

His sentiments were not taken lightly by Atwoli who in the recent past days has been keen on uniting divided Western Kenya ahead of the Building Bridges Initiative referendum.

"Anybody who can come to the conclusion that senior leaders together with the head of state can meet over a bus will surely need to see a doctor. That person can’t be normal," replied Atwoli.

The argument sparked mixed reactions from their followers with some siding with the dramatic politician as others stood in support of the vocal trade unionist.
